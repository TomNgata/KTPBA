01 Brand Essence
Primary Archetype
The Champion
Competitive drive, national pride, community through sport. Motivates through achievement and belonging.
Personality
BoldProudInclusiveCompetitiveAuthoritative
Target Audience
Kenyan bowlers (youth to adult), sports federations, sponsors, and national media. Secondary: casual recreational players and new audiences to the sport.
Tone of Voice
Commanding yet welcoming. Use active voice and strong verbs. Short, punchy headlines for sport contexts. Warm, accessible copy for community and youth. Never corporate or overly formal. Think: national stadium announcer meets community coach.
Positioning
The official governing body for ten-pin bowling in Kenya. Positioned as a legitimate, internationally-connected national sports federation that makes bowling accessible to all Kenyans while elevating performance on the world stage.

04 Logo System
Structure
Combination mark — stacked emblem above wordmark. The emblem combines: a circular arc with the Kenyan flag pattern (black/red/green + Maasai shield), bowling pins in the flag's white silhouette, and a bowling ball in flag red. Below: "KENYA" (green), "TEN PIN" (black), "BOWLING" (red — largest), "ASSOCIATION" (green).
Clear Space Rule
Minimum clear space = height of the letter "K" in the wordmark on all four sides. Never place any text, imagery, or competing graphic element inside this protected zone.

05 Visual Language
Shape Language
Strong circular forms (bowling ball, pin arc, flag shield). Sharp rectangular wordmarks. Avoid soft rounded corners in formal contexts — use 0–2px radius for authority.
Icon Style
Filled icons with bold strokes. No thin outline icons. Sport-specific iconography should be solid and high-contrast. Reference: Material Design "Filled" or Phosphor "Bold" libraries.
Pattern / Motifs
Kenyan flag tri-band stripe (black–red–green) as a horizontal rule or decorative stripe. Bowling lane gutters as parallel line textures. Circle/arc forms from the logo emblem.
Photography Style
High-contrast action photography — motion blur on the ball, sharp on the athlete's face. Warm Kenyan light tones. Authentic — real bowlers, real alleys. Avoid overly staged stock photography. Slight saturation boost on reds and greens to reinforce brand palette.
Layout Principles
Left-aligned dominant copy. 12-column grid at 1200px breakpoint. Bold typographic hierarchy over decorative elements. Generous whitespace in digital. Print materials can be more dense with stripe accents. Asymmetric layouts preferred for campaign materials.
